About

Life and Education

Growing up in the town of Menomonee Falls, Wisconsin, I first started messing around with sound design by replacing audio files in Frictional Games' Amnesia: The Dark Descent with my friends. After graduating high school, I spent a year studying at the University of Wisconsin Oshkosh location before finding my home across the country in Maine, studying audio engineering at the Husson School of Communications. During my time at School, I spent three years as President of the Technology Club where I shared my love for game development and E-Sports. Working with our advisor I was able to run workshops on the basics of both the Unity and Unreal game engines, and hold a Starcraft II tournament each semester, bringing together fellow Starcraft fans for a day of competition.

Sound Design and Game Development

Outside of school, I continued developing my audio and game development skills. I learned the basics of the Unreal Engine's Blueprint Scripting system, and I utilized middleware such as WWise and FMOD with the Unreal Engine to teach myself the fundamentals of sound implementation and event scripting. Using this knowledge, I reached out to other hobbyist devs to contribute to their projects. I spent a year with a group of devs at Thing in a Box Studios leading the audio team to creating sounds and music for their initial prototype. After my time with Thing in a Box Studios I began working on my own projects. My current project, Euphoria is a 3D puzzle platforming game designed and developed entirely by myself using the Unreal Engine.
